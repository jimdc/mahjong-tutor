Rules & Glossary

Quick definitions for tiles, sets, and the flow of a hand.

Objective

Build a complete hand before your opponents. In most rule sets, a winning hand is four melds plus one pair. The goal is to finish efficiently and score well based on the tiles and patterns you used.

Turn Flow

  1. Draw a tile.
  2. Check for a win or a strong meld.
  3. Discard one tile.
  4. Other players may claim the discard (rules vary).

Some rule sets include special calls, bonuses, or restrictions on when you can claim tiles.

Beginner Strategy

  • Favor tiles that connect into sequences or pairs.
  • Discard isolated honors early unless you already have a pair.
  • Build in one or two suits instead of spreading across all three.

Advanced Strategy

  • Track safe tiles late in the round to avoid risky discards.
  • Shape your hand to keep multiple winning paths open.
  • Recognize when to pivot from speed to value.

Exact scoring and risk management differ across rule sets (Riichi, Chinese Official, American, etc.).
