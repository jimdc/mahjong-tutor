Step 3

Glossary (Zero → Confident)

Start with zero knowledge and build up the core ideas, with chess/checkers analogies.

Tile

A tile is like a chess piece or a checker: it’s a basic unit you collect and combine. You do not move tiles on a board; you build patterns in your hand.

Suit (花色 huāsè)

A suit is like a “color” in cards or a set of similar chess pieces. Mahjong has three suits: Dots (筒 tǒng), Bamboo (条 tiáo), and Characters (万 wàn).

Dots are often red/blue (coins), Bamboo are often green (sticks), and Characters are typically red/black with the “wan” character.

Honor Tiles

Honors are special tiles outside the suits. Think of them like unique pieces in chess (not just another pawn): Winds and Dragons.

Meld (面子 miànzi)

A meld is a completed pattern of tiles, like forming a line of checkers or a tactical motif in chess. Common melds:

Chow (sequence) (吃 chī)

Pung (triplet) (碰 péng)

Kong (quad) (杠 gàng)

Pair (将 jiàng / 对子 duìzi)

Hand

Your hand is the set of tiles you hold. A winning hand is typically four melds plus one pair. Think of it as “four completed patterns + one double.”

Discard

Each turn you choose one tile to throw away. This is like choosing which chess piece to trade or which checker to sacrifice — you want to keep tiles that build patterns.

Safety (Risk)

Late in a hand, some discards are safer than others. This is like “not hanging your queen.” Safe tiles reduce the chance an opponent wins from your discard.
